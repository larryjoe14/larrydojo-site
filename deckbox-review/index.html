120mm deck box, structure

Three views of the printed body before any sculpted detail is added. The exterior is a sealed cube. The lid lifts off. A tray lifts out. Sculpting (leaves, vines, skulls, crystals — whatever direction we go) lives entirely on the outer shell, so the tray and lid can stay structural and easy to revise.

1What's locked

  • The cube footprint. 120 × 120 × 120 mm gives us room for a full standard deck plus dice, sleeved or unsleeved, with the tray.
  • Three printed pieces: outer shell, lid, lift-out tray. Each can be iterated independently.
  • The exterior is the canvas. Sculpted detail lives only on the outer shell.

2What's open

  • Tray compartment layout. The current plan shows deck well + dice well + extras well — easy to revise since the tray prints separately.
  • Lid closure. Two options shown in the detail panel: lip-and-channel (drops in, self-aligns) or recessed magnets (flush seam, snap feel).
  • Exterior sculpting direction. Not in this drawing — that's the next conversation.
